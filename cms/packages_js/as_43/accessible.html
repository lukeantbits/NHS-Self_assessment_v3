Wellbeing self-assessment

How happy are you?

Good mental wellbeing - some people call it happiness - is about more than avoiding mental health problems. It means feeling good and functioning well.

This tool uses WEMWBS, a scale often 

used by psychologists to 

measure wellbeing.

Choose the statements
that best describe your
thoughts and feelings over
the last two weeks and 

you'll get a helpful snapshot 

of how you're finding life.

Results

  • 0 - 31 points:
    Your wellbeing score is much lower than most people typically score.

    Most people have a score between 41 and 59. You may want to begin by talking to a friend or health professional about how you can start to address this. 

    There are five evidence-based steps we can all take to improve our mental wellbeing. They are:
    • Get active
    • Connect with others
    • Keep learning
    • Be aware of yourself and the world
    • Give to others
    Go to useful links for more on the five steps.

    If you are struggling because of how you feel, then your GP is a good place to find out about ways of boosting how you feel emotionally or physically.

    Your wellbeing score is lower than most people typically score.
