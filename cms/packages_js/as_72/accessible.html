Sleep self-assessment

Do you have a sleep problem?

Most people experience problems with sleep in their life. it is thought that a third of people in the UK have an episode of insomnia at some point.



This short test from Sleepio is designed to find out if you are experiencing symptoms of insomnia. It will also give you practical tips and advice for improving your sleep.

Questions

  1. Thinking about a typical night in the last month, how long does it take you to fall asleep?
    1. 0-15 minutes (Add 4 points)
    2. 16-30 minutes (Add 3 points)
    3. 31-45 minutes (Add 2 points)
    4. 46-60 minutes (Add 1 points)
    5. More than 61 minutes
  2. Thinking about a typical night in the last month, if you wake up, how long are you awake for in total?
    1. 0-15 minutes (Add 4 points)
    2. 16-30 minutes (Add 3 points)
    3. 31-45 minutes (Add 2 points)
    4. 46-60 minutes (Add 1 points)
    5. More than 61 minutes
  3. Thinking about the last month, how many nights a week do you have a problem with your sleep?
    1. 0-1 nights (Add 4 points)
    2. 2 nights (Add 3 points)
    3. 3 nights (Add 2 points)
    4. 4 nights (Add 1 points)
    5. 5-7 nights
  4. Thinking about a typical night in the last month, how would you rate your sleep quality?
    1. Very good (Add 4 points)
    2. Good (Add 3 points)
    3. Average (Add 2 points)
    4. Poor (Add 1 points)
    5. Very poor
  5. Thinking about the past month, to what extent has poor sleep affected your mood, energy, or relationships?
    1. Not at all (Add 4 points)
    2. A little (Add 3 points)
    3. Somewhat (Add 2 points)
    4. Much (Add 1 points)
    5. Very much
  6. Thinking about the past month, to what extent has poor sleep affected your concentration, productivity, or ability to stay awake?
    1. Not at all (Add 4 points)
    2. A little (Add 3 points)
    3. Somewhat (Add 2 points)
    4. Much (Add 1 points)
    5. Very much
  7. Thinking about the past month, to what extent has poor sleep troubled you in general?
    1. Not at all (Add 4 points)
    2. A little (Add 3 points)
    3. Somewhat (Add 2 points)
    4. Much (Add 1 points)
    5. Very much
  8. How long have you had a problem with your sleep?
    1. I don't have a problem (or I've had a problem for less than one month) (Add 4 points)
    2. 1-2 months (Add 3 points)
    3. 3-6 months (Add 2 points)
    4. 7-12 months (Add 1 points)
    5. Longer than a year

Results

  • 0 - 9 points:Your sleep score is very poor and the answers you have given indicate that you're experiencing a number of symptoms of insomnia. Episodes of insomnia are common, in fact, it's estimated that a third of us will struggle with it at some time in our lives. 

    There are various things you can do to help you get to sleep. This is often referred to as 'good sleep hygiene'. For example:
    • avoid caffeine later in the day
    • avoid alcohol as this is known to disturb sleep
    • avoid heavy meals late at night
    • stick to regular times to go to bed and get up
    • use thick curtains, blinds or an eye mask to stop you being woken up by light
    • try earplugs to stop any noise disturbing you
    • try taking a warm bath an hour before you go to bed
    • try listening to calming music or reading a book
    • avoid watching TV or using mobile devices in the bedroom
    However, if your sleep problems are affecting your daily life, it’s time to see your GP. You could take the results of this test to discuss with your GP or, even better, keep a sleep diary for two weeks before you go. Check useful links for a range of information and advice.
